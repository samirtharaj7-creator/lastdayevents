First Angel

Fear God, give Him glory, and worship the Creator

The first angel announces the everlasting gospel in the setting of the judgment hour. Its call to worship the Creator points directly to God's authority, His law, and the Sabbath memorial of creation.

What the message says

Revelation 14:6-7 presents a worldwide message carried to every nation, tribe, language, and people. The message calls humanity to reverence God, give Him glory, recognize the hour of His judgment, and worship Him as Creator.

This is not fear in the sense of terror alone. It is reverent loyalty, surrender, and serious attention to God because the final judgment-hour message is already sounding.

Bible anchors

  • Revelation 14:6-7: the first angel's message.
  • Daniel 8:14 and Daniel 7:9-10: sanctuary and judgment-hour background.
  • Exodus 20:8-11: worship of the Creator in the Sabbath commandment.
  • Acts 17:30-31 and Ecclesiastes 12:13-14: judgment, repentance, and accountability.

Adventist meaning

The first angel gives the foundation for the Advent movement. It joins gospel proclamation with the sanctuary message: Christ is ministering in heaven, the judgment hour has arrived, and God's people are called to live in faithful dependence on Him.

The worship language is especially important. The call to worship the Creator connects Revelation 14 with the Sabbath, because the Sabbath identifies the true God as Maker of heaven and earth.

How this is righteousness by faith

The first angel begins with the everlasting gospel, so the judgment-hour message is not a call to save ourselves by fear, performance, or religious effort. It calls sinners to stand in Christ's righteousness while He ministers for them in the heavenly sanctuary.

To fear God and give Him glory means receiving Christ so fully that His character is revealed in the life. Creator worship is therefore not merely Sabbath information; it is the surrender of the whole person to the One who both created and re-creates the heart.

Symbols and issues

  • Angel: a heaven-sent message carried by God's people.
  • Everlasting gospel: salvation in Christ, not a message of fear without grace.
  • Judgment hour: the final phase of Christ's heavenly ministry.
  • Creator worship: the Sabbath truth restored before the final worship crisis.

Warning and promise for today

The warning is that life cannot be lived as though judgment, worship, and obedience are optional. The promise is that the same message announcing judgment also carries the everlasting gospel: Christ is sufficient, present, and able to prepare His people.
